William Shakespeare

Died: 23 April 1616 (aged 52)

Years active: c.1585-1613

Occupations: Playwright, poet, actor
Language: Early Modern English
Genres: Play (comedy, history, tragedy); Poetry (sonnet, narrative, poemepitaph)
Notable works: Romeo and Juliet(1597), Hamlet(1601), Macbeth(1606), Othello (1603)

Spouse: Anne Hathaway (m. 1582)

William Shakespeare was an English playwright, poet and actor. He is widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world's pre-eminent dramatist. He is often called England's national poet and the "Bard of Avon" (or simply "the Bard").
His extant works, including collaborations, consist of some 39 plays, 154 sonnets, three long narrative poems, and a few other verses, some of uncertain authorship. His plays have been translated into every major living language and are performed more often than those of any other playwright. His characters, such as Hamlet, Othello, Macbeth, and Juliet, are iconic and continue to be studied and performed worldwide.
Shakespeare's plays, a testament to his unparalleled skill, traverse three overarching genres: tragedies, exemplified by masterpieces such as "Hamlet" and "Othello"; comedies, where works like "A Midsummer Night's Dream" and "Twelfth Night" showcase his wit and humor; and histories, including compelling narratives like "Henry IV" and "Richard III," which delve into the annals of England's past. Additionally, his collection of 154 sonnets delves into profound themes such as love, time, beauty, and mortality, showcasing the breadth of his poetic prowess.

Despite living over 400 years ago, Shakespeare's impact on literature, drama, and the English language remains profound. His plays and poems are still widely read, studied, and performed, and his influence can be seen in various forms of art and entertainment
